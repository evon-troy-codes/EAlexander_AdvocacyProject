State Policing Reforms Since The Murder Of George Floyd

While Some Progress Has Been Made. There Is Still Work Left To Be Done.

As a response community-led movements — many of which began after the death of Georgy Floyd — cities and counties have begun restructuring how local budgets and law enforcement are deployed in service of public safety. In Austin, Los Angeles, and other population centers have all pledged to cut police budgets with plans to reinvest in community programs such as supportive housing, violence prevention, and other services.
